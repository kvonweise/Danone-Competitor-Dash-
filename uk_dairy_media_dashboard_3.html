🛒 Retail Data — The Overlay Layer
Not a Channel — An Overlay Retail data is not a media channel to be activated in its own right. It's a targeting and measurement layer that sits on top of Audio, Display, OLV, CTV and DOOH — telling those channels who to reach and proving whether it worked. Dairy brands sell entirely through retail with no D2C relationship of their own, which makes this overlay the single most important data asset in the category: without it, every channel above is targeting demographics and interests rather than known shoppers.

How the Overlay Reaches Each Channel

1 — On-site sponsored products / search
Paid placement within retailer's website/app. Most established format. Performance-based; directly measurable to purchase.
2 — Overlaid on Display / OLV (off-site)
Loyalty audiences activated across open web display and video via DSP (dunnhumby Digital Offsite, Nectar360, Criteo Max). All Wavemaker CPG campaigns exceeded benchmarks.
3 — Overlaid on CTV via clean room (ITV Matchmaker / C4 + Nectar360)
Retailer data matched to broadcaster viewer IDs via InfoSum. Target "lapsed Weetabix buyers" on ITVX; measure sales uplift post-campaign via Nectar/Clubcard baskets. Fully cookieless.
4 — Overlaid across any channel via open DSP (Tesco TMI + TTD / LiveRamp)
CPGs receive Clubcard-derived audiences into their own TTD seat or agency DSP. Same audience definition applied across CTV, display, audio and DOOH simultaneously — the overlay travels with the audience, not the channel.

OLV in UK Dairy

Brand Storytelling at Scale OLV is the primary emotional storytelling vehicle for dairy brands, spanning YouTube and the open web together. Within YouTube: 6-second bumper ads for yoghurt/butter brand recall, 15–30 second skippable pre-roll for Activia gut health education, and long-form recipe/how-to content — bought via Google DV360, sitting within Google's walled garden, and typically the single largest destination within a dairy brand's OLV budget. Beyond YouTube: programmatic outstream on publisher sites (BBC Good Food, Olive, Delicious, Mumsnet); BVOD catch-up pre-roll on ITV X and Channel 4 Streaming; and short-form social video on Instagram Reels and TikTok — particularly important for challengers like Biotiful, Oatly and Yeo Valley with limited TV budgets.

CTV — Strategic Priority for Dairy

Two Reasons CTV Matters Most for Dairy Right Now 1. HFSS compliance: Flavoured yoghurts and dairy desserts restricted pre-9pm on linear TV from October 2025. CTV's post-watershed streaming audiences on BVOD/Netflix/Prime are the natural replacement — same household reach, no restriction.

2. Retailer data targeting: ITV Matchmaker (InfoSum) and Channel 4 + Nectar360 specifically enable dairy brands to activate Tesco Clubcard and Sainsbury's Nectar audiences on CTV inventory. Danone and PepsiCo brands confirmed in trials.

DOOH in UK Dairy

Grocery Proximity & Shopping Missions DOOH is powerful for dairy near grocery shopping missions. Screens near Tesco, Sainsbury's, Asda and Waitrose car parks, fuel forecourts and high streets intercept shoppers before the purchase decision. Dynamic creative responds to: weather (warm day = chilled yoghurt drink; cold = hot chocolate/dairy comfort); time of day (morning rush = breakfast occasion); proximity to stockist. Arla, Müller, Danone all use DOOH for big-format brand campaigns. Lurpak is historically a heavy OOH investor. Challenger brands use pDOOH for cost-efficient reach in key urban markets.

📺 The Trade Desk — Platform Research

CPG Path to Purchase Study

TTD Intelligence and PA Consulting's joint research (US, UK, Canada — Nov 2024, updated April 2025) found that creative emphasising brand trust, credibility and quality outperformed pure cost messaging across CPG verticals — including among shoppers who initially named price as their top priority. Even cost-aware shoppers were more likely to search for deals on favourite brands before switching to a cheaper alternative.

The research identifies three purchase journey stages — Trigger, Consideration, Purchase — and found social platforms play a strong early role, but their influence peaks later during consideration, when shoppers are actively researching and comparing.

Source: The Trade Desk Intelligence and PA Consulting, "CPG Path to Purchase," U.S., U.K., Canada, Nov. 2024 / April 2025

UK HFSS Regulation — TTD's Direct Guidance

TTD's own published guidance on the UK's Less Healthy Food (LHF) regulations states that the rules limit how many HFSS products can appear in digital advertising — explicitly naming digital audio and DOOH as having become "foundational for food and beverage advertisers" as a result.

Per TTD: digital audio builds familiarity and frequency reaching consumers "during workouts, commutes, chores, and daily routines," while DOOH "delivers immediate visibility in moments that are close to the point of sale" — language TTD uses specifically to justify reallocating dairy and food budgets toward these two channels under HFSS pressure.

Source: The Trade Desk, "Omnichannel is powering new U.K. food regulations" (thetradedesk.com)

Retail Data — Platform Capability

TTD's own retail data documentation states that brands with limited first-party data — explicitly naming CPGs — are turning to retail data to "enhance and future-proof their targeting strategies" as third-party data becomes scarce. TTD's platform provides retail sales reports and standardised attribution giving "a holistic view of online and in-store sales across many of the world's biggest retailers."

TTD explicitly contrasts this with retail media networks (RMNs) and Amazon DSP, noting RMN activations "typically have to happen in silos" tied to lower-funnel shopper marketing, while Amazon DSP "primarily reaches people who shop online" — a limitation for dairy given most purchases remain in-store.

Source: The Trade Desk, "Reach the right audience with retail data" (thetradedesk.com)

🔑 Experian — Identity & Audience Data

Digital Graph + Marketing Attributes

Experian's Digital Graph connects digital identifiers to people and households; Marketing Attributes adds demographic, interest, shopping behaviour and media preference data on top. Together they support identity resolution, cross-channel activation, analytics and measurement — positioned as the connective layer between fragmented digital signals.

Experian's identity graph is built on verified offline data spanning 126 million US households, 250 million individuals, and over 4 billion active digital identifiers — the scale that underpins match rates for CPG audience activation across CTV, mobile and display.

Source: Experian, "Announcing Digital Graph and Marketing Attributes" (Sept 2024); Experian Data Marketplace launch (Jan 2025)

CPG-Specific Audience Segments

Experian's syndicated audience library includes over 2,400 pre-built segments, directly naming CPG category-level targeting such as "Frozen Food Shoppers" and category purchase-behaviour cohorts. These activate directly on 30+ TV, social and programmatic destinations including FreeWheel, Magnite and Madhive.

Experian's Partner Audiences layer sources CPG-specific segments directly from third-party providers including Circana (purchase-based CPG data) and Dun & Bradstreet, curated specifically across the CPG, Retail and Health verticals.

Source: Experian Marketing Forward Blog, "Marketing data and identity" series (Aug 2025 release notes); Experian Data Marketplace (Jan 2025)

Retail Media Network Identity Solution

Experian launched a dedicated solution for retail media networks to improve identity resolution, ad targeting and attribution as RMNs increasingly extend into offsite media. The solution pairs Experian's identity graph (connecting addressable IDs to customers) with a third-party onboarding product to improve reach, plus an "Activity Feed" product that measures whether ad exposure drove a subsequent purchase online or in-store — directly relevant to UK dairy brands activating Tesco, Sainsbury's or Boots data offsite.

Source: Marketing Dive, "Experian launches retail media solution for networks seeking richer data" (exclusive reporting on Experian product launch)

Latest Quarterly Movement (Q4 2025)

NIQ Homescan POD data for the 12 weeks ending 27 December 2025 shows cows' dairy volumes down 0.2% YoY, but spend up 7.2%, driven by a 7.5% increase in average prices paid. Cheese remained in volume growth (+1.9%), with cheddar — 44.8% of all cow cheese volume — up 1.3%. Cream spend rose 9.7% YoY, driven by strong Christmas performance in clotted (+12.2%) and aerosol (+3.0%) variants.

Source: NIQ Homescan POD / NIQ Panel on Demand, via AHDB Q4 2025 Dairy Market Review

Private Label vs Branded Growth (Global Outlook 2025)

NIQ's global survey (17,000+ consumers, Dec 2024–Jan 2025) found 53% of respondents are increasingly buying more private label — but in the UK specifically, three retailers drove over 70% of private label growth and over 86% of branded product growth in 2024, showing retailer reputation lifts both private label and brand sales together rather than purely cannibalising branded share.

Source: NIQ, "Private Label & Brand Growth – NIQ Global Outlook 2025"

💬 YouGov — Brand Health Tracking

YouGov BrandIndex Methodology

YouGov BrandIndex is an always-on brand health tracker drawing on over 27 million registered panel members across 40+ sectors and 55+ countries. The Index score is a composite of six metrics: Impression, Quality, Value, Satisfaction, Recommend, and Reputation. For a brand to qualify for ranking reports, it needs at least 183 days of tracked data; for most-improved rankings, 548 days.

UK Biggest Brand Movers — Tracking Methodology

YouGov's UK Biggest Brand Movers report evaluates over 1,700 brands monitored in BrandIndex monthly, identifying which brands register statistically significant increases in consumer perception month-on-month across 13 metrics spanning media consumption, brand health and purchase funnel — including Buzz, Satisfaction, Recommend, Quality, Consideration, Purchase Intent and Current Customer.
